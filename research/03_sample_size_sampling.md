# Sample Size & Sampling Technique — CareConnect Research Paper

## Sample Size: 30 Participants

| Group | Profile | Count |
|-------|---------|-------|
| Group A | University students (18–25 years) | 10 |
| Group B | Working professionals (26–45 years) | 10 |
| Group C | Patients / Elderly (46+ years) | 10 |
| **Total** | | **30** |

**Justification**: Nielsen (1993) established that 5 users identify ~85% of usability issues. A sample of 30 provides sufficient statistical validity for exploratory software usability research and allows cross-group comparison.

---

## Sampling Technique: Purposive (Judgmental) Sampling

Participants were selected deliberately based on their relevance to the target user population of a healthcare management application.

**Selection Criteria**:
- Must own a smartphone
- Must have used at least one health/medicine app previously
- Must belong to one of the three defined age groups
- Willing to spend ~15 minutes testing the application

**Exclusion Criteria**:
- Healthcare professionals (doctors, nurses) — to avoid domain bias
- Participants below 18 years of age
