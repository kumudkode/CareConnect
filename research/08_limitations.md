# Limitations — CareConnect Research Paper

## 10 Identified Limitations

**L1. No Clinical Validation**
The AI responses generated by Maya (Groq LLaMA 3.3) have not been reviewed or validated by licensed medical professionals. The system is educational, not diagnostic.

**L2. AI Hallucination Risk**
Large language models can generate plausible but medically inaccurate information. Users may not always be able to identify incorrect AI responses.

**L3. Internet Dependency**
All AI features (Maya chat, prescription scanning, report analysis) require a stable internet connection. There is currently no offline AI capability.

**L4. Image Quality Dependency**
The accuracy of prescription scanning depends entirely on the clarity of the uploaded photograph. Blurry, poorly lit, or handwritten prescriptions may yield incorrect results.

**L5. No Real-Time Drug Interaction Database**
CareConnect does not connect to a live pharmaceutical database (e.g., DrugBank, OpenFDA). Drug interaction warnings are AI-generated and not database-verified.

**L6. English-Only Interface**
The current version supports English only, limiting usability for patients who are more comfortable in regional languages such as Hindi, Tamil, or Bengali.

**L7. Small User Study**
The usability evaluation was conducted with 30 participants, which may not represent the full diversity of the target user population across India.

**L8. No Regulatory Clearance**
CareConnect has not obtained regulatory approval from CDSCO (India) or FDA (USA). It cannot be used as a certified medical device or diagnostic tool.

**L9. Firebase Vendor Lock-In**
The application is tightly coupled to Firebase services (Auth, Firestore, Storage). Migrating to a different backend provider would require significant code changes.

**L10. Lack of Real Patient Data**
Development and testing used simulated/mock data. Real-world performance with actual patient prescriptions and medical reports at scale remains untested.
