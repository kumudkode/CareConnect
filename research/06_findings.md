# Findings — CareConnect Research Paper

## 5 Key Research Findings

---

**Finding 1: AI Assistance Significantly Improved Prescription Understanding**

87% of participants (26/30) reported that Maya's AI explanations helped them understand their prescribed medicines better than reading the prescription alone. The prescription scanning feature received the highest helpfulness rating of 4.43/5, indicating strong practical value for patients.

---

**Finding 2: Medication Tracking Became Measurably Easier**

90% of participants (27/30) found the digital medicine inventory and reminder system significantly more convenient than paper-based tracking. The reminder feature scored 4.47/5 — the highest across all evaluated features — suggesting it directly addresses the medication non-adherence problem identified in the research problem.

---

**Finding 3: AI Assistant Reduced Healthcare Information Complexity**

Non-medical participants rated Maya's responses 4.17/5 for relevance and clarity. Particularly notable: working professionals (26–45 age group) found the AI explanations of medical report values most useful, as they could understand lab results without scheduling an extra doctor visit.

---

**Finding 4: Users Preferred Multimodal Interaction**

73% of participants (22/30) used both image upload and text chat in combination, rather than using only one input method. This validates the design decision to support multiple input modes (text, image, voice) and confirms that multimodal AI interaction is preferred for health-related queries.

---

**Finding 5: Cloud-Based Storage Improved Health Record Accessibility**

83% of participants (25/30) appreciated the ability to access their prescriptions and medical reports from any device. This finding supports the use of Firebase cloud storage over local-only storage solutions and highlights the demand for portable, persistent health records among all age groups.
